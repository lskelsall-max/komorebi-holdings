Our Strategy

Travel & Transport is the ultimate complex adaptive system.

A $10.9 trillion market, close to 10% of global GDP, employing one in ten people worldwide — and on the edge of reinvention.

The System
More than the sum of its parts

Travel and transport spans hospitality, distribution platforms, mobility providers, and the infrastructure that supports them. Hotels, resorts, lodges, and rentals are tied together by the digital systems that connect supply with demand. The sector works as a complex system rather than a set of silos.

Changing demographics, wealth creation, technology, and regulation all act as feedback loops that shape how people and goods move. Underneath it lies a constant driver: the human desire to connect, trade, and explore.

Mobility covers the modes of transport — aviation, shipping, rail, and roads — now being reshaped by electrification, autonomy, and emerging air mobility. Infrastructure anchors the system with airports, ports, rail lines, toll roads, and logistics hubs. Taken together, this network forms the circulatory system of the global economy: capital intensive, politically sensitive, indispensable, and on the edge of reinvention.

Secular Tailwinds
Permanent drivers of investment demand

Four structural forces are reshaping travel and transport. None of them are cyclical.

Rising Global Middle Class

Unlocking new flows of business and leisure travel across emerging and developed markets, with the greatest growth concentrated in Asia, Africa, and Latin America.

Digitisation

Booking platforms, distribution systems, artificial intelligence, and smart infrastructure are rewiring how travel is experienced and how transport is managed at every level.

Global E-Commerce

Driving sustained capital needs in ports, private airports, logistics corridors, and rail networks as the physical backbone of digital commerce.

Electrification

Creating new forms of mobility — eVTOLs, hybrid marine, EV fleets — and reshaping the infrastructure backbone of the future of transport and energy.
